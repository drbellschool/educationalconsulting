# The Mind's Eye Product Design Report

## Product Direction

The Mind's Eye should be a standards-first classroom simulation platform, not a fantasy RPG with history pasted on top.

Teachers choose a town, historical window, subject, TEKS, class size, accessibility supports, and mastery rules. The system then assembles a historically grounded learning experience from primary sources, Sanborn maps, OCR text, photographs, and AI-generated instructional scaffolds.

## Core Experience

Students enter a local historical moment and participate through structured role-play, investigation, evidence collection, discussion, writing, and decision-making.

The game layer should come from:

- uncertainty
- evidence
- collaboration
- public decisions
- visible dice checks
- classroom consequences
- mastery demonstrations

The product should use language like:

- simulation
- investigation
- hearing
- newsroom
- expedition
- reconstruction
- town council
- inquiry mission

Avoid making the product a direct D&D clone. Dice mechanics can be inspired by tabletop role-play, but the instructional identity should be school-safe and original.

## First Scope

The first scope should support grades 7-12 Texas core subjects.

### Core Subjects

- English Language Arts and Reading
- Mathematics
- Science
- Social Studies

### Initial TEKS Sources

- 19 TAC Chapter 110: ELAR
- 19 TAC Chapter 111: Mathematics
- 19 TAC Chapter 112: Science
- 19 TAC Chapter 113: Social Studies

## First Prototype

**Prototype:** Texarkana Historical Simulation Engine

The teacher selects:

- town: Texarkana
- year or date range
- subject
- TEKS
- class size
- number of mastery demonstrations required
- accessibility/accommodation presets

The system produces:

1. source packet
2. teacher approval screen
3. student-facing simulation
4. map-linked locations
5. role cards
6. dice-check moments
7. vocabulary and translation supports
8. evidence notebook prompts
9. mastery dashboard
10. teacher source notes

## Historical Integrity Rule

Every generated mission must separate:

- verified historical facts
- reasonable source-based inferences
- fictional instructional/gameplay additions

This is not optional. It is the trust layer of the product.

## Design Principle

The archive is the ground. AI is the guide. The teacher is the authority. The student is the investigator.
