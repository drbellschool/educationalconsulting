# Community and Institution Engine

## Purpose

The Community and Institution Engine models churches, schools, civic groups, newspapers, local government, aid networks, and community concerns.

This gives the town a social life beyond commerce and law enforcement.

## Institution Types

- churches
- schools
- newspapers
- city council
- county offices
- courts
- sheriff's office
- fire companies
- fraternal organizations
- charity groups
- rail/depot offices
- hospitals/doctors/druggists
- libraries or reading rooms where historically supported

## Church and Community Quest Sources

Churches and community institutions can generate quests from:

- bulletins if historically available
- newspaper church notices
- prayer concerns when source-supported or teacher-created
- charity drives
- illness concerns
- funerals and memorials
- community meals
- school events
- building repairs
- missing persons/livestock notices
- aid for travelers
- moral/civic dilemmas

## Important Caution

Do not assume every historical church had a printed bulletin.

If no bulletin source exists, the system may create a historically plausible church concern, but it must be labeled as simulation_element or creative_license.

## Institution Record

Each institution should include:

- institution ID
- name
- type
- map location ID
- known leaders if sourced
- active years if known
- source citations
- community role
- possible quest types
- review status
- sensitive content notes

## Quest Hooks

Examples:

- a church needs help delivering supplies
- a school needs missing materials recovered
- a newspaper asks students to verify a rumor
- a city council needs evidence before voting
- a civic group wants a public notice written
- a doctor needs help tracing a health concern
- a community organization asks for help with travelers arriving by train

## TEKS Connections

Community institutions support:

- civic participation
- local government
- community problem-solving
- informational text
- argument and evidence
- public speaking
- cause and effect
- ethics and citizenship

## Review and Approval

Community reviewers should be able to:

- verify institution names
- add local context
- flag sensitive topics
- approve or reject AI-generated institution uses
- distinguish sourced institutional records from plausible simulation additions

## Non-Negotiable

Community life must be handled respectfully and source-aware, especially when religion, race, tragedy, crime, or family history are involved.
