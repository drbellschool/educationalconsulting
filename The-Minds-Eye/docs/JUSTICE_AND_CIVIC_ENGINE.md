# Justice and Civic Engine

## Purpose

The Justice and Civic Engine models sheriff offices, deputies, courts, judges, warrants, testimony, hearings, fines, restitution, and civic responsibility in age-appropriate instructional ways.

The goal is not to make crime entertaining. The goal is to teach civic process, evidence, ethics, and consequences.

## Core Institutions

- sheriff's office
- deputies/constables where historically appropriate
- jail
- courthouse
- judge
- clerks
- city council
- witnesses
- newspapers reporting civic matters

## Scenario Types

- warrant assistance
- missing property investigation
- witness interview
- court hearing
- evidence presentation
- public complaint
- restitution agreement
- false accusation correction
- civic ordinance discussion
- jury-style evidence review

## Good vs Bad Guy Simulations

The system may support clear conflict scenarios, but must avoid shallow stereotypes.

Better framing:

- evidence-based investigation
- conflicting testimony
- civic responsibility
- harm repair
- justice process
- community safety

## Student-Triggered Justice Consequences

If students act badly inside the simulation, the justice system can respond.

Examples:

- harassment of an NPC
- property damage
- theft attempt
- false accusation
- trespassing
- disorderly conduct in a public setting

Possible consequences:

- sheriff warning
- temporary story-world detention
- court appearance
- testimony requirement
- apology/restitution
- loss of time
- reputation damage
- teacher-approved reset

## Teacher Approval Required

Major justice escalation must require teacher approval.

Examples requiring approval:

- arrest/detention language
- courtroom scene
- accusation of serious crime
- jail reference
- violence-related scenario
- sensitive historical crime topic

## Source Requirements

Justice scenarios should use:

- newspapers
- court records where available
- city directories
- Sanborn map locations
- local histories
- verified officeholders when possible

If unsupported by sources, label as simulation_element or creative_license.

## TEKS Connections

Justice scenarios support:

- civic process
- rights and responsibilities
- argument and evidence
- source evaluation
- point of view
- public speaking
- ethical reasoning
- cause and effect

## Non-Negotiable

The system must never present AI-generated accusations about real historical people as verified unless there is a clear source citation.

When in doubt, fictionalize names or label clearly as creative_license.
