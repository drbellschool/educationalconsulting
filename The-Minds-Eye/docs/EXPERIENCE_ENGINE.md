# Experience Engine

## Purpose

The Experience Engine connects real classroom experiences to the historical simulation.

Some learning should happen outside the digital town, then feed back into the world.

Examples:

- science labs
- field observations
- water testing
- gardening experiments
- engineering builds
- weather measurements
- debates
- speeches
- writing workshops
- art/design products
- CTE demonstrations

## Core Principle

Real-world academic work can change the simulation.

Students do something in class, collect data, submit results, and the digital town responds.

## Science Integration

Science teachers need labs connected to content.

The platform should allow teachers to create or select labs that produce data students report back into the simulation.

### Water Testing Example

Students test water samples for:

- pH
- turbidity
- temperature
- dissolved oxygen
- nitrates where appropriate
- visible contaminants

They report results to the game as data about local water supplies.

Possible simulation effects:

- doctor notices illness concern
- town council discusses water quality
- business near creek is investigated
- students recommend a public health action
- map marks risky water source

### Gardening Technique Example

Students test:

- compost vs no compost
- water amount
- soil type
- sunlight
- mulch
- seed spacing

They report results.

Possible simulation effects:

- crop yield changes
- local market prices shift
- church food drive succeeds or struggles
- business inventory updates
- town asks for an agricultural recommendation

### Engineering Example

Students build and test:

- bridge models
- water filters
- pulley systems
- load-bearing structures

Simulation effects:

- wagon bridge can open
- town approves construction
- freight route improves
- railroad delay is solved

## Experience Record

Each real-world experience should include:

- experience_id
- teacher_id
- class_id
- subject
- TEKS_ids
- lab/activity title
- instructions
- materials
- safety notes
- data fields
- artifact requirements
- simulation effect rules
- teacher approval status

## Data Submission

Students or teachers can submit:

- numeric lab results
- written observations
- photos when allowed
- tables
- calculations
- conclusions
- recommendations

## Simulation Effect Rules

The teacher should approve how real-world data affects the town.

Effects may include:

- unlock quest
- update location status
- update inventory
- update commerce supply
- update NPC concern
- trigger town meeting
- create newspaper request
- create court/council evidence need

## Assessment Connection

Experiences create assessment artifacts.

Examples:

- lab report
- data table
- scientific explanation
- engineering notebook
- recommendation letter
- public health notice
- town council presentation

## Teacher Controls

Teachers can:

- make an experience required or optional
- choose whether data changes the world
- approve student-submitted data
- attach rubrics
- connect experience to mastery evidence
- choose whether AI gives feedback

## Non-Negotiable

The simulation should not replace hands-on learning. It should give hands-on learning a meaningful world-facing purpose.
