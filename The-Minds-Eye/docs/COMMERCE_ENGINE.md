# Commerce Engine

## Purpose

The Commerce Engine makes the town feel alive.

Businesses should not be static labels on a map. They should have inventories, shortages, demand, customers, deliveries, prices, and relationships to the railroad and local institutions.

## Core Commerce Data

Each business can include:

- business/location ID
- business type
- owner/operator if known
- source citation
- goods/services offered
- typical inventory
- price table
- supply sources
- customer groups
- railroad dependency
- seasonal demand
- risk factors
- review status

## Commerce Tables

The system should maintain tables for:

- general store goods
- livery services
- blacksmith services
- hotel/boarding house costs
- newspaper/printing services
- pharmacy/druggist items
- railroad freight costs when known
- livestock and feed
- tools and repair items
- food staples
- school/church/community supplies
- court/sheriff office fees where historically appropriate

## Source Status

Prices and inventories must be labeled:

- verified_source: supported by a source
- source_based_inference: inferred from nearby records or period data
- simulation_element: plausible for play
- creative_license: invented for engagement

## Commerce-Generated Quests

Commerce can generate scenarios such as:

- delayed shipment
- missing invoice
- counterfeit money concern
- business shortage
- damaged goods
- delivery route problem
- price dispute
- unfair trade accusation
- railroad freight confusion
- inventory needed for church/school/civic event
- supply chain disruption

## Student Uses

Students may:

- buy supplies
- trade items
- deliver goods
- inspect invoices
- calculate cost and change
- compare prices
- investigate shortages
- connect commerce to geography and railroads
- use evidence to resolve disputes

## TEKS Connections

Commerce supports:

- economic systems
- supply and demand
- geography
- math computation
- reading primary source advertisements
- informational text analysis
- argument and evidence
- civic responsibility

## Teacher Controls

Teachers can:

- simplify prices
- disable money mechanics
- approve commerce quest branches
- set math level
- require citations for claims
- require written evidence before purchases or negotiations

## Non-Negotiable

Commerce should create learning opportunities, not turn the simulation into shopping for its own sake.
